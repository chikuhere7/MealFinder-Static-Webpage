Vegetarian


Vegetarianism is the practice of avoiding meat and sometimes other animal products in one‚Äôs diet. People who follow this lifestyle are known as vegetarians. There are different types of vegetarian diets, such as lacto-vegetarian (includes dairy), ovo-vegetarian (includes eggs), and vegan (excludes all animal products). Many individuals choose vegetarianism for ethical reasons, believing it is wrong to harm animals for food. Others follow it for health benefits, such as reducing the risk of heart disease, obesity, and diabetes. Environmental concerns also motivate people, as plant-based diets generally have a lower carbon footprint than meat-based diets. Some religions like Hinduism, Jainism, and Buddhism also encourage vegetarian living. A vegetarian diet includes fruits, vegetables, grains, legumes, nuts, and plant-based proteins. However, vegetarians must ensure they get enough essential nutrients like protein, iron, and vitamin B12. Overall, vegetarianism promotes a more compassionate and sustainable way of living.
